About Me

Dr. Zully Lizarazo is a licensed Clinical Psychologist in the state of California. She holds a doctorate degree from William James College with a specialization in forensic psychology and Latinx mental health. Over the years, she has worked in a range of clinical and forensic settings—from state hospitals and correctional institutions to international placements in Ecuador and Costa Rica. She has conducted numerous forensic evaluations, including competency to stand trial assessments, in both English and Spanish. Currently, Dr. Lizarazo serves as the Drug Abuse Program Coordinator at the Metropolitan Correctional Center in San Diego where she continues to conduct forensic evaluations for Spanish-speaking individuals within the federal court system. Dr. Lizarazo is also known for her work on the translation and cultural adaptation of the Massachusetts Incompetence to Stand Trial Questionnaire and her research in the use of psychological assessments for Spanish-speaking populations, including the use of interpreters during forensic evaluations. Dr. Lizarazo is driven by a commitment to cultural competence, ethical forensic practice, and increasing access to mental health services for underserved communities.
